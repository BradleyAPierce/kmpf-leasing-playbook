Credit Submission Process

This section explains where to submit credit requests and outlines what information is required. Following this process ensures timely approvals and smoother transactions.

Types of Submissions
    Primary Method: CRM | Salesforce Portal
    Standard Process: All credit submissions should be made through CRM | Salesforce
    Emergency Alternative: Phone or email requests (use sparingly)

Required Information

Initial Requirements:

  • Leasing Partner
  • Total Deal Value (including Buyout)
  • Lease Payment
  • Payout Terms
  • Purchase Option Type
  • Processing Center
  • Account Info (pre-populated)
  • Owner Info (pre-populated)

Additional Requirements:

  • 2 years of audited financials/tax returns
  • Interim financials for current and previous year-over-year term

Credit Challenged/New Customers:

  • Signed credit application with personal guarantee required

Quote Generation Process

This describes available KMPF quote types, and when to use them. Each quote type represents a different financial strategy to support customer goals and manage cash flow. Upgrade quotes tend to strengthen long-term account relationships.

Upgrade and Buyout Quotes
    Access Method: Request directly from within Salesforce
    Delivery: Immediately downloadable or emailed shortly after request.
Quote Type When to use Customer Benefits
Upgrade
to Return 		When the customer is ready to refresh older devices and prefers not to retain ownership. Provides a device refresh. Satisfies the financial obligation of the existing lease. Can be less costly for customer than other quotes.
Upgrade
to Keep 		When the customer wants new equipment but wishes to retain select existing devices. Maximizes use of retained devices, and provides a refresh of other devices. Satisfies the financial obligation of the existing lease, plus covers the residual value of the devices.
Buyout
to Return 		When customer cannot finance with existing KMPF Partner, and must finance with a new KMPF Partner, or another lender. Customer does not wish to incur the cost of equipment ownership. The customer may request this quote if they plan to leave KM.
Buyout
to Keep 		When customer cannot finance with existing KMPF Partner, and plans to own the equipment. Customer satisfies their intent to own the equipment.
Partial
Quote 		When customer wants to remove specific devices from the existing lease. Allows customer to retain some existing devices and pay a reduced monthly amount for the remaining term of the lease.
IMPORTANT: Customer-requested buyout quotes will not be issued directly to customers without notice (protection mechanism).

Funding Package Requirements

Details the documentation needed for a deal to ship and fund successfully. Completing these requirements correctly avoids hold-ups and ensures customer delivery stays on schedule.

  • Signed and dated lease agreement (complete signature box with legal name, Federal Tax ID, printed name, title, and date) KM Order Agreement
  • Detailed Funding Sheet (DFS)
  • Any related upgrade or buyouts
  • Complete Funding Package Checklist
  • Sales Tax Exemption Certificate (if applicable)
  • All upgrade/buyout quotes (even if $0 amount)
  • Quote must be valid for at least 5 days at submission
  • Include Equipment Removal Authorization form for KM pickup machines
  • Include KMPF partner communication for cross-partner upgrades
  • Promotional forms (if applicable)

Special Programs Procedures

Covers guidelines for specialized programs like municipal leases Tax-Exempt Lease Purchase (TELP) and master agreements. This section helps you navigate unique deal structures correctly from the start.

TELP/Municipal Leases
  • Disclosure Requirement: Must disclose TELP pursuit at time of approval
  • Form Requirement: Customer must provide IRS Form 8038G or 8038GC prior to funding
  • Limitation: $10MM annual cap per end user
  • Note: First Citizens Bank (FCB) does not offer TELP program; lease filled out by KMPF partner, not KM
Master Lease Agreements
  • System: Standard lease document in CPQ functions as master agreement
  • Benefit: Negotiate terms once, write subsequent business without renegotiation
