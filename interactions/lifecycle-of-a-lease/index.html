Lifecycle of a Lease

The Lifecycle of a Lease Wheel gives you a quick, clear view of how a lease moves from the very first conversation to renewal. Each stage shows what needs to happen, who's involved, and how your actions keep the process moving.

Understanding this lifecycle helps you ask better questions, set the right expectations, and guide your customer with confidence. When you know how the lease process works from start to finish, you can avoid delays, improve the customer experience, and uncover more upgrade and renewal opportunities.

Identify Opportunity for a Solution and/or Equipment Lease

Actions Needed

  • Identify an opportunity for a solution, and a lease.
  • Ask questions about process for acquisition of technology equipment.
  • Obtain information about needs and preferences for an equipment lease.

Create Opportunity

Actions Needed

  • Create opportunity in Salesforce.
  • Identify decision maker.
  • Identify decision maker contact information.
  • Identify possible products based on the customer's need.

Customer Credit Worthiness

Actions Needed

  • Contact a KMPF partner to pre-qualify the customer for credit.
  • Submit credit application to leasing company.

Review Credit Decision

Actions Needed

  • If credit is approved, go to DETERMINE TYPE OF LEASE.
  • If credit is not approved, reach out to Regional Leasing Manager (RLM).
  • RLM will advise on next steps or options for the customer.

Determine Type of Lease

Actions Needed

  • Determine the type of lease best suited to the customer.
  • Applies customer's answers to questions to determine lease type best suited to customer situation:
    • Fair Market Value lease
    • Dollar Out lease

Need for Bundled Lease

Actions Needed

  • Determine if the lease will be:
    • Payment for hardware only.
    • Payment for hardware, maintenance, and supplies all bundled together.
  • Apply customer answers to leasing questions to determine this.
  • Describe the benefits of bundling hardware, maintenance, and supplies together in one lease.

Build Quote

Actions Needed

  • Work with sales manager to prepare a quote. Launch CPQ tool from the Opportunity in Salesforce to generate a quote.
  • Presents quote to customer. If customer accepts quote, go to SUBMIT LEASE PAPERWORK.
  • If customer does not accept quote, investigate customer concerns with the quote, and follow up with the sales manager.

Submit Lease Paperwork

Actions Needed

Work with Sales Manager on lease documents or generate lease documents from CPQ.

Submit lease documents to customer for signature.

IF CUSTOMER REQUESTS CHANGES TO LEASE DOCUMENTS

Consult with sales manager or RLM to resolve any customer requests for changes to lease documents.

IF CUSTOMER SIGNS LEASE DOCUMENTS

Following customer signature, initiate the order.

Lease Documents to KMPF Partner

Actions Needed

  • Transmit lease document package to the KMPF leasing partner.
  • KMPF partner reviews package.

Ship and Fund

Actions Needed

  • If lease package satisfactory, Konica Minolta receives Ship and Fund from KMPF partner.

Installation and Invoicing

Actions Needed

  • Confirm delivery and installation of equipment.
  • Confirm that KMPF partner has sent first invoice to customer.

Customer Follow Up to Review Needs

Actions Needed

  • Meet with customer RE lease approximately 12 months prior to lease expiration.
  • Start discussion to explore customer needs and options for the end of the lease.

Present Options to Renew Lease

Actions Needed

  • Present options to renew lease:
    • Upgrade
    • Buyout
  • Based on customer input, develop quote for either upgrade or buyout.
